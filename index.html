Exergames & EF

This study explored the viability of exergames (physical active video games) as an alternative form of exercise for children with developmental delays. Additionally, this project examined the effect an active video game may have in boosting executive function in this special needs population.

  • Date: June - August 2014
  • Partners: Ramapo for Children, Inc NYU McSilver Institute for Poverty Policy and Research

Mobile Movement + Math (M3)

M3 explored the role of gestures in the development of conceptual thinking in the context of mathematical fractions. The goal of the project is to develop a mobile game application that ultimately allows the user to embody their understanding of fractions.

  • Date: 2015 - 2016
  • Partners: Institute for Learning Technologies, Columbia University NSF

Learning to Code: Traditional vs. Game-based

The current pilot study examined the effect of collaborative-based learning (paired programming) on the development of computational thinking skills in middle school students learning how to code in a traditional teaching module or a game-based teaching module.

  • Date: 2015 - 2016
  • Partners: Institute for Learning Technologies, Columbia University Roads to Success CodeCombat

Microcity Programmers

This project is designed to explore the use of adaptive code modality in an educational coding game to assess the impact on student coding knowledge acquisition and how to encourage for computational thinking practices during gameplay. Pilots involve user testing to assess game design and gameplay experience, survey data and gameplay data will be collected, and short semi-structured interviews will inform directions for study 2.
